We often approach problems by analogy—"how did someone else solve this?" While useful, this method inherits the assumptions and limitations of previous solutions. First principles thinking offers an alternative: strip away assumptions and rebuild from fundamental truths.

## What Are First Principles?

A first principle is a foundational proposition that cannot be deduced from any other proposition. It's the bedrock upon which everything else is built.

Consider Elon Musk's famous battery example:

> "People would say, 'Battery packs are really expensive and that's just the way they'll always be.' And I said, 'What are batteries made of?' Cobalt, nickel, aluminum, carbon, polymers... Break it down on a material basis and ask, 'What is the spot market value of these materials?' It's like $80 per kWh. So clearly you just need to think of clever ways to take those materials and combine them."

## The Three-Step Process

1. **Identify Assumptions** - What do you believe to be true about the problem?
2. **Break Down to Fundamentals** - What are the absolute basic truths?
3. **Create New Solutions** - Build up from fundamentals, unconstrained by convention.

## When to Use It

First principles thinking is computationally expensive. Don't use it for everyday decisions—that would be exhausting. Save it for:

- Problems where conventional solutions aren't working
- Industries ripe for disruption
- Personal beliefs you've never examined

The goal isn't to abandon all prior knowledge, but to ensure your reasoning stands on solid ground.
